The Impact of Technology in Early Childhood Education
Site Author
 Lynn University


Abstract
 This site provides the impacts of technology in early childhood education, while also helping the reader understand the pros and cons of the integration of technology in education. It provides reliable statistics from UNICEF and other sources that demonstrate the prevalence of cell phone usage in children depending on their age range, as well at the detrimental effects of that said usage. While acknowledging that some technology use may be beneficial for a child's education, for example, for research purposes, it highlights the negative effects to warn readers and prevent them from allowing technology to consume the lives of their child.

72% of U.S. High School Teachers Say Cellphone Distraction Is A Major Problem In The Classroom
Site Author
 Pew Research Center


Abstract
 This site has real teachers sharing their experience with cell phone usage in the classroom. based on the title, its clear that a large percentage of teachers are reporting cell phone usage as a major problem for students. The site always provides statistics based on surveys conducted on teachers, students, and school districts, each sharing shocking results. The authors demonstrate that what's thought of as an easy solution isn't necessarily helpful, as teachers are struggling to properly enforce new cell phone policies in class. The article finished by showing that teens themselves believe that the pros of cellphone usage outweigh the cons, which is a sad and false opinion.
