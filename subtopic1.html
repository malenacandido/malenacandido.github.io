Technology and Your Child's Developing Brain
Site Author
 The Schreiber Pediatrics


Abstract
 This site dives deep into the numerous risks your child has when using excessive technology. The authors break down the following risks into seven categories that each provide detailed explanations of what is happening to the brain for those who may not be as educated in developmental processes. The seven categories are listed as followed: Dopamine Overload, Executive Function Skills, Heightened Stress Levels, Myelin Development, Social Skills and Empathy, Social and Emotional Development, and lastly Control and Perseverance. The site also stresses the important of traditional interactive play such as reading and playing with others that an positively affect the development of a child's brain.

Screen Time Statistics Reveal How Parents Use Screens as Babysitters, Educators, and Entertainment Tools
Site Author
 Lurie Children's Blog


Abstract
 This site gives reliable and surprising statistic in relation to technology use, or more specifically, screen time on individual devices. The authors provide research on the relationship between technology and parents, who in recent years have been using technology to keep their children buy rater than getting daycare, for example. The site also provides percentages of parents who regret using as much screentime as well as regrets allowing their child to use screens for as often as they do. It also is very helpful into breaking down the most used services children use on their devices, that fall into categories such as YouTube, Games, and Educational Apps.
